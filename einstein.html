Who is Einstein?

Quick Summary

Albert Einstein is a mathematician, physicist, and science author from German. Was born on March 14, 1879 in Ulm, Württemberg, German and died on April 18, 1955 in Princeton, New Jersey, U.S. A genius son of Hermann Einstein and Pauline Koch who was well known by the world with his relativity theories. Einstein won a Nobel Prize for Physics in 1921 for his photoelectric effect explanation.

Einstein always appeared to have a clear view of the problems of physics and the determination to solve them. He had a strategy of his own and was able to visualize the main stages on the way to his goal. He regarded his major achievements as mere stepping-stones for the next advance.

Einstein’s gifts inevitably resulted in his dwelling much in intellectual solitude and, for relaxation, music played an important part in his life. He married Mileva Maric in 1903 and they had a daughter and two sons; their marriage was dissolved in 1919 and in the same year he married his cousin, Elsa Löwenthal, who died in 1936.

Einstein's Lifetime

  • (1879) Was born in Ulm, Württemberg, German.
  • (1885) Moved to Munich, German.
  • (1901) Gained his diploma from Swiss Federal Polytechnic School in Zurich. Acquired Swiss citizenship. Accepted a position as technical assistant in the Swiss Patent Office.
  • (1903) Married Mileva Maric.
  • (1905) Obtained his doctor's degree.
  • (1908) Appointed Privatdozent in Berne.
  • (1909) Became the Professor Extraordinary at Zurich.
  • (1911) Became the Professor of Theoretical Physics at Prague.
  • (1912) Returned to Zurich to fill a similar post.
  • (1914) Became a German citizen. Remained in Berlin.
  • (1919) The marriage with Mileva Maric was dissolved. Einstein married Elsa Löwenthal.
  • (1920) Started to embark on the construction of unified field theories.
  • (1921) Win a Nobel Prize for Physics by his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
  • (1933) Renounced his citizenship for political reasons. Emigrated to America to take a position of Professor of Theoretical Physics at Princeton.
  • (1940) Officially become the citizen of United States.
  • (1945) Retired from his post.
  • (1955) Was die in Princeton, New Jersey, United States.

Most Important Works

  • (1916) General Theory of Relativity
